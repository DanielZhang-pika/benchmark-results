💎 BEST VALUE CHAMPION
Best Value: Qwen3.5-397B
At just $0.39/1M tokens, Qwen3.5-397B ranks #4 overall (77.6%) — only 1.4pp behind Claude Opus at 13× lower cost. The strongest cost-performance ratio in the benchmark.
⚡ SPEED + COST SWEET SPOT
Fastest Top Performer: Kimi K2.5
Kimi K2.5 combines the lowest latency in the top tier (0.74s avg) with strong accuracy (76.1%) at $0.60/1M. The go-to choice for latency-sensitive real-time applications.
🏆 BUDGET TIER
Cheapest High Performers: $0.20/1M Tier
Both Qwen3-235B (76.9%) and Grok 4.1 Fast (75.7%) deliver near-premium accuracy at only $0.20/1M — 25× cheaper than Claude Opus. Ideal for high-volume deployments.
⚠️ MINIMAX GAP
MiniMax Underperforms Expectations
MiniMax M2.7 (71.3%) and M2.5 (72.0%) rank 18th–19th, trailing top models by ~7pp. Upgrading to Qwen3.5-397B or Kimi K2.5 offers significant quality improvement at similar cost.
🔴 LOCAL MODELS NOT READY
Open-Weight Models Not Production-Ready
gpt-oss-120B scores only 59.6% — a 19pp gap behind the top tier. For agent skill routing specifically, open-weight models currently cannot match cloud APIs in routing accuracy.
✅ HALLUCINATION SAFETY
Top Models Are Hallucination-Safe
All top-10 models achieve hallucination rates below 0.25%, well within the 1% deployment threshold. Llama 4 Maverick is the only model blocked (1.38%). Claude, Gemini, and Qwen all show zero or near-zero hallucination.

Cost-Performance Tradeoff
Scoring
Final Score = 0.7 × W-CatAvg + 0.3 × PEQ
Difficulty-weighted category accuracy + synonym-aware parameter extraction quality.
W-CatAvg = Σ w(i) × category_accuracy(i)
Difficulty-weighted mean — harder categories (lower mean accuracy) get higher weight. w(i) = (1 − mean_acc(i)) / Σ(1 − mean_acc(j)).
CatAvg (shown as reference) = unweighted mean of 13 categories, aligned with BFCL standard.
